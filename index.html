Everyday bras are the best option for everyday wear.

    Purpose:
  • to be worn under any type of clothing
  • provide comfortable support for regular wear

Bralettes are casual bras that do not have wires and no padding.

    Purpose:
  • provide a little bit of modesty
  • for those who do not want to wear a bra with any wires

Sports bras are best used for when you want to do exercise.

    Purpose:
  • provide support to restrict movement of breasts to minimize pain
  • reduce breast damage from vigorous exercise

Strapless bras do not have shoulder straps, they are usually worn with strapless dresses or tops. Some are known as convertible bras, and these are strapless bras that are designed to have removeable straps.

    Purpose:
  • fashion over function
  • hide the illusion that someone is wearing a bra while providing relative support

Did you know?

Women wore corsets in the 14th century to the late 19th century. Due to the shortage of metal in World War I, it encouraged end of corsets and the development of the modern brassiere.

Did you know?

Early corsets were constructed with baleen, the thin strips found in the "teeth" of baleen whales. Baleen were referred to as "whalebone". They were an ideal for corsets due to the flexible nature of the material.

Did you know?

Women who played tennis at Wimbledon in 1887 wore corsets made of whalebone and metal, which stabbed them so much, they were covered in blood.

Did you know?

Men may wear bras as well. Some may wear them to handle gynecomastia, the development of breast tissue. While some other men may wear bras to cross-dress, and some others may wear sports bras to deal with nipple chafing during long distance runs.

Dr. Elena Bodnar designed and created a bra that can be converted into a face mask. She was inspired to do so after working with victims of Chernobyl. Clean air can be breathed in through a butterfly valve sewn into the lining. Only available from sizes 32B to 40C.

Did you know?

Based on a survey in 2008, it was found that around 80% of women were wearing an incorrect bra size. This may be due to the lack of information and resources about proper sizing available for women. It may also be that brassieres are a taboo as it can represent a sexual symbol.

Finding the right bra size

The shoulder straps should not be digging into your shoulders, nor should it be slipping off.
Breasts should fit into the cup of the bra, it should not be spilling out or have a significant gap. The band of the bra should be secure around the rib cage while parallel to the ground and not riding up the back.
Here are the steps to take to find your bra size.

Bust measurement. Measure the fullest part of your bust, measuring tape is parallel to the ground and is smug. Not loose or binding. Select the option closest to your measurement.
Band measurement. Should be snug and parallel to the ground. Exhale to get the smallest measruement possible. Select the option closest to your measurement.
